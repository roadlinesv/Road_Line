Cancellation Policy

Last revised: June 2, 2025

Guest cancellation

If a guest wants to cancel a booked trip, we encourage them to notify their host as soon as possible via Turo messaging and to process the cancellation through the Turo website or mobile application. See instructions for how to cancel a trip. The cancellation is effective immediately. Whether the guest receives a full refund, a partial refund, or receives no refund depends on the circumstances, including when the guest cancels the trip, the length of the trip, and the trip type. The cancellation period is based on the vehicle’s time zone.

Full refund

Guests receive a full refund if a cancellation occurs during the free cancellation period, is a cancellation for cleaning or safety concerns, or is the result of a mutual agreement to cancel between host and guest supported by evidence.

Trip modifications

If a guest requests a trip modification and the request is approved, that modification does not reset the free cancellation period for the trip. It remains tied to the original booking time.

Cancellation for cleaning or safety concerns

Guests may cancel free of charge and receive a full refund if, upon inspecting the vehicle at trip start, they have concerns that it hasn't been cleaned or is unsafe. Guests are only eligible for a full refund if they: do not accept the vehicle, do not check in for the trip, and immediately contact Customer Support to report the issue.

Partial refund

Guests receive a partial refund if a cancellation occurs outside the free cancellation period, for incomplete post-booking steps, or a guest no-shows for a trip.

No refund

There are no credits/refunds issued for early returns except when the guest has submitted a trip modification request through the Turo website or app, as defined in the Turo terms of service, to shorten their trip and the request is approved. There are no credits/refunds issued for non-refundable trips cancelled after the grace period of 24 hours after booking.

Guest trips canceled by host, host no-shows, and trips canceled by Roadline

If a host cancels a guest’s trip or is a no-show, Turo will refund the guest in full. Funds from the canceled or no-show trip will be available for 24 hours after the cancellation so a guest can rebook their trip. If the funds remain unused after 24 hours, we'll automatically issue a full refund.

Guest trips impacted by flight or train delays or lost baggage

If a guest’s flight or train is delayed or canceled, or if a guest experiences an issue with their baggage upon arrival, they must message their host to request a trip modification. If the host cannot accommodate a new start time, Turo will issue a full refund if documentation is provided.

Host cancellation and no-shows

Hosts who cancel a trip less than 24 hours before start time are subject to a $50 fee; if more than 24 hours, $25. No-shows incur a $150 fee. All-Star hosts may be excused from cancellation fees.

Trips canceled by guests (host side)

If a guest cancels outside the free period, hosts receive a portion based on trip length. For non-refundable trips canceled after 24 hours, hosts receive their earnings less Turo fees.

Guest no-shows

If a guest no-shows, hosts may receive 75% to 200% of a day's average cost depending on trip duration. Hosts must report no-shows within 72 hours to be eligible.

Trips canceled by Roadline

In some cases, Roadline may cancel trips. Hosts may receive part of the trip earnings depending on timing. Documentation and good faith attempts to reschedule are required for compensation.
